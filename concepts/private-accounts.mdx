---
title: "Private Accounts"
description: "Understand the privacy-layer balance scope used across Arcane integrations."
---

A private account is the application-level scope for private balance. It lets your product refer to a user, employee, customer, card program, payroll treasury, or application wallet without exposing low-level protocol state.

## What it represents

| Product context | Private account meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Card funding | Cardholder, card funding profile, or program treasury |
| Payroll | Employee, employer treasury, payroll program, or contractor |
| Treasury | Internal operating wallet or settlement pool |
| Wallet app | User-controlled private balance |

## What it hides

The private account abstraction keeps these details out of product code where possible:

- UTXO selection.
- Commitments.
- Nullifiers.
- Merkle witnesses.
- Proof inputs.
- Encrypted outputs.

Your backend or Arcane SDK still needs those details internally. Your product should not make them user-facing concepts.

## Design choice

Start with one private account per product balance scope. Avoid sharing a private account across unrelated products or legal entities.

For example, a card product and a payroll product can use separate private accounts even when they belong to the same end user.
