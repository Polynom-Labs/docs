---
title: "Commitments and Nullifiers"
description: "Understand the low-level privacy-pool concepts behind Arcane transactions."
---

Commitments and nullifiers are protocol-level concepts. Most product integrations should not expose them to users.

## Commitments

A commitment represents private value that was created inside the privacy layer. Observers can see that a commitment exists, but they should not learn the owner, amount, or product context from the commitment alone.

## Nullifiers

A nullifier prevents double spending. When private value is spent, the protocol reveals a nullifier derived from private data. Observers can check that the same private value is not spent twice without learning the original private details.

## Why product code should abstract them

Your product should work with:

- Product records, such as card loads, payroll cycles, payouts, and treasury operations.
- Managed wallet references.
- SDK operation history.
- Transaction signatures.
- Status and reconciliation records.

The SDK and protocol handle:

- Commitment creation.
- Nullifier generation.
- Merkle proof construction.
- Proof generation.
- State updates.

## When you need to care

You may need commitment and nullifier details when debugging low-level SDK behavior, auditing protocol state, or building custom chain adapters. Most partner integrations should keep these details behind SDK calls and backend tooling.
