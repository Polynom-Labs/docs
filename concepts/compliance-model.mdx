---
title: "Compliance Model"
description: "Understand the compliance boundary for Arcane integrations."
---

Arcane is designed for applications that need both private transactions and a controlled compliance path.

It does not make every transaction public. It creates structured ways to reveal only the data that an authorized workflow permits.

## Compliance boundaries

| Boundary | Responsibility |
| --- | --- |
| Partner application | User onboarding, product policy, sanctions controls, business rules, and customer support |
| Arcane privacy layer | Private state, proofs, encrypted audit payloads, indexing, and disclosure support |
| Auditing Portal | Case requests, approvals, scoped review, reports, and access logging |
| Chain network | Public transaction settlement and state availability |

## Common review cases

- Suspicious or anomalous activity.
- Card funding dispute.
- Payroll dispute.
- Regulator request.
- Internal compliance audit.
- Legal hold or investigation.

## Implementation guidance

- Keep product references stable.
- Do not put sensitive user data on-chain.
- Route disclosure through approved cases.
- Log report generation and downloads.
- Separate operational support access from auditor access.
- Define retention and export policies before production launch.
