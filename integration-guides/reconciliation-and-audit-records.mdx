---
title: "Reconciliation and Audit Records"
description: "Link Arcane private activity to partner product state without making all activity public."
---

Arcane integrations need two views of the same activity:

- A simple product view for users and operations.
- A permissioned audit view for compliance, support, disputes, and reporting.

Reconciliation connects those views without leaking private transaction details publicly.

## Reconciliation keys

Store stable references on both sides of the integration.

| Key | Owner | Purpose |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `external_reference` | Partner | Links Arcane activity to your product object |
| `private_account_id` | Arcane and partner | Identifies private balance scope |
| `funding_intent_id` | Arcane and partner | Tracks public funding into private state |
| `private_transaction_id` | Arcane and partner | Tracks private movement |
| `audit_record_id` | Arcane and partner | Enables scoped disclosure |
| Public transaction hash | Chain | Confirms funding, withdrawal, or settlement |

## What to record

For each user-visible money movement, record:

- Product object id.
- Arcane resource id.
- Amount, asset, and chain.
- Status history.
- Public transaction hash if one exists.
- Audit record id.
- Webhook delivery ids.
- Retry attempts and error codes.

## Audit record access

An audit record should not automatically expose all private details. It should be accessed through a controlled workflow.

| Actor | Typical access |
| --- | --- |
| Customer support | Limited status and product reference |
| Application administrator | Approve or deny scoped disclosure |
| Auditor | Review approved transaction fields in an active case |
| Organization administrator | Manage users, applications, and access boundaries |

See [Controlled Disclosure](/integration-guides/controlled-disclosure) for the approval path.

## Operational reports

Common reports include:

- Funding intents by status.
- Private transfers by product reference.
- Withdrawals by destination chain.
- Failed or delayed shielding operations.
- Audit cases and approved disclosures.
- Card-load or payroll-cycle reconciliation.

## Design guidance

- Treat Arcane ids as durable references.
- Keep product ids private to your backend and audit metadata.
- Do not put personally identifiable information in public chain data.
- Make status updates append-only when possible.
- Use idempotency for all product ledger updates.
