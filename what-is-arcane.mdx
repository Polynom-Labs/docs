Arcane is infrastructure for financial organizations that need to operate
on-chain without making sensitive transaction activity broadly visible.

It combines confidential transaction capabilities with controlled access,
reporting, and auditability. Clients can add privacy to financial products
while preserving a governed path for authorized review.

<Info>
  **Arcane in one line:** confidential transaction infrastructure with a
  governed path for authorized review.
</Info>

## Why Arcane

Public blockchains provide shared settlement and verifiable transaction
history, but their transparency can expose balances, payment amounts, and
patterns of financial activity.

Financial organizations need a different operating model. Sensitive
transaction details should remain confidential during normal use, while
approved compliance, audit, support, and investigation workflows must still be
able to access the specific evidence they are authorized to review.

Arcane is designed around this separation: privacy for the transaction path and
governed visibility for the review path.

The two products address different responsibilities. The Privacy Layer protects
the transaction flow. The Compliance Platform governs when and how authorized
users can review private evidence.

## What Arcane adds to a client product

| Requirement | Arcane capability |
| --- | --- |
| **Transaction confidentiality** | Private transfers, balances, and protected financial activity. |
| **Controlled visibility** | Access to specific evidence based on purpose, scope, role, and approval. |
| **Auditability** | Review cases, reports, and activity records for authorized workflows. |
| **Product integration** | Infrastructure and integration tools that connect to an existing client application. |

Arcane does not replace the client's customer experience, business logic,
ledger, or system of record. It adds privacy and governed review capabilities
to the client's financial product.

## Designed for financial workflows

Arcane can support products and operations such as:

- wallet and stablecoin payments;
- payroll, payouts, and partner distributions;
- treasury and corporate payment operations;
- confidential tokens and digital-asset workflows;
- financial actions embedded in client applications.
